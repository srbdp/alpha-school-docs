First-hand accounts from parents, students, and public recognition.

## Response

<ResponseField name="parent_testimonials" type="array">
  Parent accounts documented from public sources.

  **Example:** `[{"speaker": "Scott Jensen", "quote": "It's working for all the kids at the school"}, {"speaker": "Anonymous Parent (Astral Codex Ten)", "quote": "Genuine progress but marketing claims overstated"}]`
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="student_testimonials" type="array">
  Student accounts documented from public sources.

  **Example:** `[{"speaker": "Elle Kristine", "quote": "Friends at traditional school are so stressed out"}, {"speaker": "Marshall (age 9)", "quote": "The app gives you an explanation of how to get it correct"}]`
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="public_recognition" type="array">
  Awards and recognition received.

  **Example:** `[{"award": "Most School of the Future School", "organization": "Austin Chronicle (Best of Austin)", "year": 2024}]`
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="selection_bias_warning" type="string">
  Important context about testimonial representativeness.

  **Example:** `"Testimonials from public sources create inherent selection biasâ€”satisfied families more likely to share publicly"`
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="critical_testimonials_available" type="boolean">
  Whether critical perspectives exist in public testimonials.

  **Example:** `true`
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="critical_testimonial_summary" type="string">
  Summary of critical perspectives in testimonials.

  **Example:** `"Anonymous parent notes: not genuine 2-hour learning (3-4 hrs actual), not AI-driven as marketed, estimates approach benefits 30-70% of children"`
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="testimonials_vs_evidence_distinction" type="string">
  How testimonials should be weighted.

  **Example:** `"Testimonials represent satisfaction and perception, not verification of academic claims"`
</ResponseField>
